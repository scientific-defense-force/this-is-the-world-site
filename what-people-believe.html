What People Believe

People refers to those most likely to visit this site. Those who have the technology and the free time to be interested in such a topic. Which is to say the 'machine operators' or the WENAO (Western Europe North America Oceania + Japan) middle class.

'People' correctly understand that there are wealthy and poor counties. The key misunderstanding is the reasons for this difference and the relationship between the poor and wealthy countries.

The First World

  • Democracy - Democracy is the key reason for the wealth of the first world. The democratic process keeps corruption in check and enables prosperity.
  • Free market capitalism - Primarily democracy protects free market capitalism, which allows for the generation of new wealth through new ideas and technology.
  • Freedom - Key to this is the concept that people are free to choose their employer or to start a business themselves.

The Rest

  • Corruption - The poorer countries either have corrupted democracies or are varients of dictatorships.
  • Restricted markets - The political corruption leads to corrupted markets. Those with new ideas cannot compete with established businesses and thus advancement and the raising of wealth is stymied.
  • Oppression - The people have limited freedom and are further prevented from fighting for improvements to their countries through invidual effort.

Is This Belief Valid?

These beliefs are exagerated but not invalid. Still they miss the key reason for the existence and continuation of poor and rich countries. Why?

The core misunderstanding is that the most important difference is the countries internal makeup. There is the implication that if the poor countries could replicate the political systems of the wealthy countries they could be similarly successful.

Through capitalism becoming globally dominant and the advance of globalization the factor that determines if your country is rich or poor is where it fits into the global system. Or more to the point it is where the corporations in your country rank on the global scale.

Through globalization businesses now compete at a global level. You could apply a progressive democracy to a poor country but due to economies of scale and technological advantage there is no way that local business in those countries can compete with the likes of Apple, Google or Microsoft. They often can't even compete in terms of food production despite the advantage of being local and having cheaper distribution costs.

Regardless of their political makeup, they need to make money in the global system. What do they have? They have a large number of poorly educated poor people who need work and can work cheaply. Their only real option is to facilitate these people being employed by foreign companies for low wages.

The Rich Countries Fight To Persist This Arrangement

The poor countries are doubly unable to improve their position in the global system as the wealthy countries actively work to suppress their advancement.

Free trade agreements favor the rich countries

There is a long string of trade agreements that favor the already wealthy countries. The poor countries are excluded and thus are further disadvantaged. The OECD is the primary organization that works towards the benefit of the rich countries.

Direct support for reigimes that facilitate globalization

Primarily this is the USA (and it's closest allies) influencing the poor countries to avoid leadership that may put up barriers to globalization. Interestingly often the supported regimes are highly undemocractic or corrupt. This includes support for regime changes which can be greatly destructive for the people of the country.

Corporate political influence

What is true is that these poor countries are more corrupt than the rich countries. They are further unable to improve their global standing due to corporations conducting lobbying to influence these countries to their own benefit usually as the expense of the countries people.

There Isn't Capacity For More Rich Countries

It is estimated that 4 earths worth of production would be required if everyone consumed as many resources as those in the US. The poor countries cannot all raise their own status and become rich countries, because the current global system is designed to function with a large number of poor people to work for low pay.

For everyone in the world to live like the WENAO middle class there would have to be a very different system where most of the wealth in the world doesn't go to the top 1%.

What About China?

Hasn't china managed to raise it's wealth within the global system? Doesn't that prove that the poor countries are limited by their political systems?

China is an interesting example. It has managed to significantly expand it's econonmy since the 1950s. It achieved this primarily through government investing in infrastructure and in assisting local business to be competitive. Part of this being the choice to ignore global patent law for the good of the chinese.

However, whilst very effective at it China is still a provider of relatively poor people for employment by rich countries. It is entirely dependent on them and is not set up to replace the current set of global corporations.
